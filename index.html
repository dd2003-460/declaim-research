Research Statement

My research program aims to develop a quantitative framework for understanding how knowledge evolves, propagates, and creates value. It integrates three pillars:

  • Theoretical Foundation: A monograph arguing that understanding, Bayesian updating, proof, and intuition are all instances of pattern matching at different compression ratios (Shannon + Kolmogorov)
  • Empirical Pipeline: Detecting paradigm shifts as structural breaks in scientific discourse using LDA topic modeling (1,500 arXiv abstracts across cs.AI, cs.CL, cs.CV, quant-ph) + Bayesian changepoint detection
  • Application Layer: Information-theoretic analyses of social phenomena (vaccine hesitancy as control group failure, financial NLP at FinNLP 2026)

WS #1: Understanding as Compression (Ch2)

Establishes the information-theoretic foundation: Kolmogorov complexity as a model of cognition, dual-process theory as compression at different scales, and the unified framework connecting mathematical proof, Bayesian updating, and scientific intuition.

WS #2: Probability as Approximate Inference (Ch5)

Demonstrates that probability theory emerges as the unique consistent framework for reasoning under incomplete information. Draws on the Cox-Jaynes derivation, Koopman's submarine search problem (1946), and empirical cases from Base Rate Neglect to COVID-19 social behavior. Three-mode unification framework.
